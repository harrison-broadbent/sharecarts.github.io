How to Share Kroger Cart

Kroger is one of the largest grocery store chains in the United States. You can shop in-store or online for fresh produce, snacks, drinks, household supplies, and even medicine. Many people enjoy using Kroger’s online shopping because it saves time and helps them plan better.

But what if you want to share your Kroger cart with someone else? Maybe you’re meal planning with a roommate. Maybe your spouse wants to add items to the grocery list. Or maybe your family is helping you shop for a party. Whatever the reason, sharing your cart makes shopping easier.

The only problem? Kroger’s website does not include a built-in “share cart” feature. Luckily, there’s an easy solution using this browser extension ↗.

In this guide, you’ll learn how to share a Kroger cart, why it’s helpful, and the easiest way to do it using a free tool ↗. Don’t worry—it's simple and only takes a few seconds to get started.

Why Share Your Kroger Cart?

There are lots of reasons to share your Kroger cart with someone:

  • Meal Planning with Family or Roommates - If you live with other people, you probably share food and groceries. Sharing your cart lets everyone add their own items, like their favorite cereal, snacks, or drinks.
  • Group Events or Parties - Planning a birthday party or cookout? You can build the grocery cart together. One person starts the cart, shares it, and others add chips, drinks, napkins, or dessert.
  • Helping Parents or Grandparents - Older family members may not feel comfortable shopping online. You can create the cart for them and send it over. They can look at what you picked and make changes or check out when ready.
  • Splitting Costs - Maybe you and a friend are both buying groceries for the week. Sharing one cart helps you keep track of what’s shared and how to divide the bill.
  • Making Sure You Don’t Forget Anything - When two or more people build the cart, it’s easier to catch missing items. Someone might remember to add laundry detergent or eggs that you forgot.

Can You Share Your Kroger Cart?

Yes! Even though Kroger doesn’t have a built-in “share cart” feature, it is still possible.

The best way to do it is by using a Chrome extension called Share Amazon Cart ↗. This tool helps you turn your cart into a link that you can share with anyone. There are also other ways like using lists or copying product links, but the Chrome extension is the most straightforward way on Kroger.

Using the “Share Amazon Cart” Chrome Extension

What It Is

The Share Amazon Cart Extension ↗ is a free tool for Chrome and Edge users. It lets you create a link from your Kroger cart. When someone clicks the link, they’ll see all the items and can add them to their own cart with one click.

Share from the main Kroger site, or any of the Kroger brands.

The extension requires minimal permissions and installs quickly.

How to Use the Extension

  1. Install the extension. Go to the extension page ↗ and install it on Chrome or Edge.
  2. Shop like usual. Add grocery items to your Kroger cart like you normally would.
  3. Click "Share Cart". Open any Kroger page and click the new “Share Cart” button.
  4. Copy & Share. Copy the shareable link and send it to someone.

The recipient opens the link and adds the same items to their cart.

Note: It does not clear anything previously in their cart.

Manual Process

  • Open each item in the browser.
  • Copy the URL from your browser.
  • Paste each link into a message.

This works if you only have a few products. It’s not ideal for large or complex carts. You risk sending the wrong link or missing an item, which can cause confusion for the people you’re sharing with.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about sharing a cart on Kroger. Have a different question and can’t find the answer you’re looking for? Reach out here and we’ll get back to you as soon as possible.

Can I share my Kroger cart with someone?
Yes, you can share your Kroger cart with anyone you want. This includes your friends, family, roommates, co-workers, or even with parents and students.
On which sites can I share a Kroger cart?
The main Kroger.com ↗ site and all Kroger brands (20+), such as: Smith's Food and Drug ↗, King Soopers ↗, Ralphs ↗, QFC ↗, Pick'n Save ↗, Pay-Less Super Markets ↗, Metro Market ↗, Mariano's ↗, Jay C Food Stores ↗, Gerbes Super Markets ↗, Fry's Food Stores ↗, Fred Meyer ↗, Foods Co ↗, and Food 4 Less ↗.
Does my shared cart link stay updated or synced?
No, the link reflects your cart items at the time it was created. If you change the items in your cart, you should use the extension ↗ from this guide to create a new link.
Do I need to be signed in?
Yes, you must be logged into Kroger to share or receive a cart.
Is an extension required to receive the cart?
No, you do not need an extension to receive a Kroger cart—only to share one.
Can I send my cart to another account?
Yes, these same steps can be used to send Kroger cart or transfer Kroger cart to another account.
Can I share my whole Kroger cart?
There is no limit to how many total items you can send using the extension. Kroger may limit the total quantity that can be purchased for certain items.
Will the cart links I create expire?
No, the links stay active indefinitely and are accessible on all desktop and mobile browsers. Product availability may vary.
Will the other person see my address or payment info?
No! The cart link only shows the item names and prices. Your name, address, and credit card info stay private.

Conclusion

Sharing your Kroger cart is a great way to make shopping easier for everyone. Whether you live with roommates, shop with your family, or plan events with friends, sharing helps everyone stay on the same page. You don’t have to copy and paste items or send long messages about what to buy.

The Chrome extension ↗ is the easiest and fastest way to do it. It takes just a few clicks and saves you a lot of time. If you don’t want to use an extension, lists and copy-pasting links also work, though they take more effort.
