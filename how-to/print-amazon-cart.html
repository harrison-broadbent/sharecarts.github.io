How to Print Amazon Cart

You may run into a situation where you need to print your Amazon cart. This means making a paper copy or saving it as a file (like a PDF). There are many reasons for this. You might want to show the list to someone else, keep a record for future shopping, or use the list to compare prices at other stores. Even though Amazon doesn’t give you a direct “print” option for your cart, there are easy ways to do it.

One of the best ways is by using a free browser tool like the Share Amazon Cart Extension ↗. It helps you organize, share, and print your cart in just a few clicks. In this article, we’ll explain why someone might want to print their Amazon cart and how this tool makes the process simple and fast.

Why Print Your Amazon Cart?

  • To share with someone – Maybe you’re picking gifts and want a friend or family member to look at your list. Printing your cart makes it easy to show them.
  • To compare prices – You can take your printed list to a store and check if the items are cheaper there.
  • For records – Sometimes, people want a hard copy of what they planned to buy, especially if they're buying for work or school.
  • To plan ahead – Maybe you’re not ready to buy yet, but you want to remember what you picked out. Printing your cart helps you keep track.

Can You Print Your Cart on Amazon?

Yes! You can either:

  • Use the extension Share Amazon Cart ↗
  • Press Ctrl + P (or Command + P on a Mac)

Amazon doesn’t have a “print cart” button, but you can still do it by pressing Ctrl + P (or Command + P on a Mac), and choose to print the page or save it as a PDF.

The Best Way: Use the "Share Amazon Cart" Extension

The best way to print your Amazon shopping cart is to use the Chrome Extension ↗. Normally, when you print a cart on Amazon, it includes extra spacing and information you don't need on paper. This tool prints out all your cart items in one click, formatted with only the product information.

How to Use the Extension

  1. Install the extension. Go to the Share Amazon Cart extension page ↗ and install it on Chrome or Edge.
  2. Shop like usual. Add items to your Amazon cart like you normally would.
  3. Click "Share Cart". Open any Amazon page and click the new “Share Cart” button.
  4. Print Cart. Click the print cart button.

Why It’s Better Than Other Methods
